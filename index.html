Optimization

1. Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)

  • GD is infeasible for large datasets so we need SGD.

$i$ is a random index between 1 and n:
$$
w^t=w^{t-1}-\eta_tg_{t-1},
$$
where $w^t$ is the weight at iteration t, $\eta$ is the learning rate at iteration t and $g_t=\nabla L(w^t; x_i, y_i)$ is the gradient of $i$th example’s loss function with respect to $w^t$. The $w^t$, $g_t$ could be a scalar, vector or matrix with same size.

2. Mini-batch Gradient Descent

$i_1,\ldots,i_m$ are random indices between 1 and n:
$$
w^t=w^{t-1}-\eta_t\sum_{j=1}^mg^{i_j}{t-1},
$$
where $g^{i_j}_t=\sum{j=1}^m\nabla L(w^t; x_{i_j}, y_{i_j})$ is the gradient of $i_1$th to $i_m$th example’s loss function with respect to $w^t$.

  • mini-batch GD is less noisy than SGD.

3. Momentum

  • Oscillation is not good for optimization.
